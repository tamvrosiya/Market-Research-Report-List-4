Toothed ring forceps have small grooves or teeth on the tips to provide a stronger grip on tissues, while toothless ring forceps have smooth tips for a gentler touch on delicate tissues. Surgeons choose between these two types based on the specific needs of the procedure and the type of tissue being manipulated.</p></p>
