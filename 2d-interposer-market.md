<p><p>2D interposer technology is widely used in the semiconductor industry for integrating multiple chips on a single substrate. There are three main types of 2D interposers: silicon, organic, and glass. Silicon interposers offer high performance and reliability but are more expensive. Organic interposers are cost-effective and flexible but may have limitations in terms of performance. Glass interposers provide good electrical performance and high density but are less widely adopted due to their high cost and difficulty in manufacturing. Each type of interposer has its own unique advantages and limitations for various applications.</p></p>
