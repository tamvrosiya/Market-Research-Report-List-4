<p><p>Atomic emission spectroscopy is a technique used to analyze the elemental composition of a sample by measuring the light emitted when atoms are excited. The main types of atomic emission spectroscopy are flame atomic, spark atomic, arc atomic, inductively coupled plasma atomic, and others. Flame atomic utilizes a flame as the excitation source, spark atomic uses an electric spark, arc atomic uses an electric arc, and inductively coupled plasma atomic uses a plasma torch. Each type has its own benefits and applications in various industries, such as environmental analysis and material testing.</p></p>
