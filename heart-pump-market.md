<p><p>A heart pump, also known as a ventricular assist device (VAD), is a mechanical pump that helps support the heart in pumping blood throughout the body. These devices are commonly used in patients with severe heart failure or those awaiting a heart transplant. The global heart pump market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 14.7% during the forecast period.</p><p>The increasing prevalence of cardiovascular diseases, along with the growing aging population worldwide, is driving the growth of the heart pump market. Technological advancements in heart pump devices, such as the development of smaller and more efficient pumps, are also contributing to market growth. Additionally, the rising awareness about heart health and the increasing adoption of minimally invasive surgeries are further fueling the demand for heart pumps.</p><p>One of the latest trends in the heart pump market is the increasing adoption of continuous flow pumps over pulsatile flow pumps. Continuous flow pumps are more durable, quieter, and have a longer lifespan compared to pulsatile flow pumps. Moreover, the growing acceptance of destination therapy, where the heart pump is used as a permanent treatment rather than a bridge to transplant, is also a significant trend in the market.</p></p>

<p><p>Heart pump market consists of two main types: Implantable heart pump devices and Extracorporeal heart pump devices. Implantable heart pump devices are surgically implanted into the patient's chest to assist with pumping blood, while Extracorporeal heart pump devices are external devices used temporarily to support heart function during surgeries or to stabilize patients until a suitable donor heart is found for transplant. Both types of devices play a crucial role in managing heart failure and improving patient outcomes.</p></p>
