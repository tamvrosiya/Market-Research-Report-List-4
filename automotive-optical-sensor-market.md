<p><p>Automotive optical sensors can be categorized into three main types: Analog sensors, Digital sensors, and Others. Analog sensors provide continuous output signals proportional to the quantity being measured, offering high sensitivity and accuracy. Digital sensors convert light intensity into binary signals for digital processing, providing precise detection and easy integration with digital systems. The category of Others includes specialty optical sensors designed for specific applications, such as color detection or distance measurement, offering unique functionalities beyond traditional analog and digital sensors.</p></p>
