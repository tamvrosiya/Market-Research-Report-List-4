<p><p>Core materials for composites are essential components that provide structural support and rigidity to the final composite product. Two common types of core materials used in composites are foam cores and honeycombs. Foam cores are lightweight, easily moldable, and offer good thermal insulation properties. Honeycombs, on the other hand, are made of hexagonal cells arranged in a honeycomb-like structure, providing great strength and stiffness to the composite. Both foam cores and honeycombs are widely used in various industries such as aerospace, automotive, and construction.</p></p>
