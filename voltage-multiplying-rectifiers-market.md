<p><p>Voltage-multiplying rectifiers are used to increase the output voltage of a power supply. The half-wave voltage doubler configuration utilizes a diode and a capacitor to double the input voltage. On the other hand, the full-wave voltage doubler uses two diodes and two capacitors to achieve the same result. Both types of rectifiers are commonly used in applications where a higher output voltage is required, such as in power supplies for electronics or in voltage multipliers for specialized equipment.</p></p>
